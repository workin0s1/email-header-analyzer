How Email Travels Across the Internet – Explained with Headers

When an email is sent, it goes through multiple servers before reaching the recipient. These steps are stored in the header:

  1. Sender’s client connects to their SMTP server
  2. Mail relays across intermediate servers
  3. Each hop is logged in “Received” lines
  4. Final delivery happens to recipient’s inbox
